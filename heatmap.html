Why NC is #1: The November 2023 Medicaid expansion added ~600,000 newly eligible adults in NC — many of whom have untreated SUD. The treatment infrastructure hasn't caught up. With an active IMD waiver, no CON law, above-average Medicaid rates, and the largest population in the Southeast, NC is the single best expansion target. Charlotte, Raleigh-Durham, Greensboro, and Asheville (high addiction prevalence) all represent viable facility locations.

Ideal target: Acquire an existing licensed facility in Charlotte or the Triad (Greensboro/Winston-Salem) — avoid de novo builds given fast-moving market. Target: 60–80 beds, ASAM 3.5 + 3.7, established MCO contracts.

Tier 2–3 State Analysis
Georgia (78) — BUY with conditions
Why buy: 11M people, active IMD waiver, Atlanta as a referral hub, above-average SUD rates, rural North Georgia is severely underserved.
Condition: Georgia has a CON (Certificate of Need) law for healthcare facilities — adds 6–18 months and $50K+ to new bed approvals. Acquire an existing licensed facility; avoid de novo. Medicaid expansion is partial (Gateway program), limiting eligible population vs. full expansion states.
Tennessee (76) — BUY
Why buy: 45/100K OD death rate — one of the highest in the Southeast. Active IMD waiver. No CON law. Nashville is a growing metro with corporate employer base (commercial payer opportunity). Memphis has extremely high SUD rates and limited treatment.
Caveat: TennCare (state Medicaid) does not have full Medicaid expansion, limiting the eligible pool. Rates are moderate. Strong BDR needed to build commercial mix.
Kentucky (74) — BUY
Why buy: Full Medicaid expansion + active IMD waiver + highest OUD prevalence in the Southeast at 12.1%. Significant federal grant funding (SAMHSA, HRSA) flows to KY providers. Louisville and Lexington are viable anchors.
Caveat: CON law. Existing provider density slightly higher than pure opportunity states (more competition). Geographically further from Stoa's LA base.
Mississippi (72) — BUY (rate caveat)
Why buy: Directly adjacent to Louisiana. Active IMD waiver. Only 1.4 facilities per 100K — most underserved state in the analysis by this metric. Cultural continuity with Stoa's Baton Rouge operation is seamless.
Critical caveat: Mississippi has NOT expanded Medicaid. Lower reimbursement rates (~$200–240/day for 3.5 residential) — significantly below Louisiana's $335/day. Payer mix will be worse. Only viable with strong commercial development strategy.
West Virginia (66) — Watchlist
Why watch: 82/100K OD death rate — the national epicenter. Medicaid expanded. IMD waiver active. Enormous need. Good rates.
Why not yet: 1.8M population (small market absolute). Market more saturated than it looks per 100K because the population base is tiny. Very rural. CON-free but challenging to staff with nurses/clinical in rural WV. Geographic isolation from LA makes operational oversight harder.
Texas (41) — AVOID
Why avoid: Texas does not have a broad 1115 IMD exclusion waiver. For a 112+ bed facility, Medicaid simply cannot pay for residential SUD stays. Texas Medicaid (STAR) is also not expanded. The business model that works in Louisiana does not work in Texas without a fundamentally different payer strategy (private pay + commercial only). 30M people creates the illusion of opportunity — but the revenue engine is broken without the waiver.
RECOMMENDED ACQUISITION SEQUENCE — 5-YEAR PLATFORM BUILD
Ranked by risk-adjusted opportunity. Assumes Louisiana base stabilized and 1115 waiver renewed.
ACQUISITION 1 — YEAR 1–2
Arkansas (Little Rock)
Geographic adjacency, Medicaid expansion, no CON, severely underserved (1.8 facilities/100K), cultural continuity with LA. Stoa's exact playbook replicates. Low risk, fast execution. Target: 50–70 bed facility or de novo. Est. stabilized revenue: $3.5–4.5M.
ACQUISITION 2 — YEAR 2–3
North Carolina (Charlotte or Triad)
Largest market in analysis. Post-2023 Medicaid expansion means massive unmet need wave. Active waiver, strong rates, no CON. Higher complexity (larger market, more competition) but enormous upside. Target: acquire existing licensed facility, 60–100 beds. Est. stabilized revenue: $5–7M.
ACQUISITION 3 — YEAR 3–4
Georgia (Atlanta suburbs) or Tennessee (Nashville)
Both offer large markets + active waivers. Georgia's CON law makes TN slightly easier operationally. Nashville has commercial employer base for payer mix diversification. Either market creates a 3-state, 4-facility platform primed for strategic exit at 10–12× EBITDA.
Platform exit thesis: Louisiana + Arkansas + North Carolina + Georgia/Tennessee = 4 facilities, ~300 beds, $18–22M combined revenue, $6–8M platform EBITDA at 35%+ margin. Strategic buyer exit (Acadia Healthcare, BrightSpring, Universal Health Services) at 10–12× = $60–96M enterprise value. All four states in confirmed IMD waiver states with Medicaid expansion or strong rate structures. Geographic corridor: Gulf Coast → Southeast → Atlantic seaboard.
