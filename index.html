▶ How Each Initiative Gets Executed
1. Billing & Collections — $109K saved
The problem: The business pays 5% of gross collections to an outsourced biller — top-of-market for behavioral health. The current vendor's clean claim rate and denial rate are unknown, which means there may be additional revenue leakage beyond the fee itself.

Step 1 (Week 1–4): Pull an A/R aging report. Identify how much is 60/90/120+ days outstanding. Every dollar over 90 days has a 30–50% probability of never being collected. This is the first number you need.

Step 2 (Month 1–2): Issue an RFP to 3 behavioral health billing specialists — Netsmart, Waystar, and one boutique SUD-specific firm. Benchmark rate: 3.0–3.5% of net collections vs. current 5%. Renegotiating alone saves $109K/yr with zero operational change.

Step 3 (Month 4–6): Evaluate in-house billing. One certified billing specialist ($55K salary, $75K fully-loaded) handling $7M+ revenue = effective rate of 1.0%. Combined with AI RCM tools (see AI section), this gets the blended billing cost under 2% — saving $215K+/yr vs. current spend.

Key metric to track: Clean claim rate (target 95%+), denial rate by payer, days-to-collect by service line, and cost-per-claim. Set monthly vendor scorecard.
2. Contract Labor — $126K saved
The problem: $246K/yr in agency/contract labor = ~12–13% of clinical payroll. Agency rates for LPNs in Louisiana run $50–70/hr vs. $30.50/hr for direct employees — a 65–130% premium. This is the "fire department" problem: you're paying emergency prices for routine coverage.

Step 1 (Month 1): Audit which shifts are consistently filled by agency. Is it always weekend nights? Specific positions? If it's predictable, it's solvable with better planning — not emergency agency calls.

Step 2 (Month 1–3): Build an internal per diem float pool. Hire 2–3 per diem LPNs at $35/hr (no benefits required for under 30hrs/week) who commit to 2 weekend shifts/month. Upfront cost: ~$5K in sign-on bonuses. Annual savings vs. agency: $80K+.

Step 3 (Month 2–4): Partner with LSU School of Nursing and Southern University at Baton Rouge for clinical placements. Nursing students cover non-licensed tasks under supervision — free labor for patient transport, vitals, documentation support during peak hours.

Step 4 (Ongoing): Retention program. LPN turnover nationally runs 30–40%/yr. At Stoa's LPN count, that's 8–10 replacements/year at $5–8K each in recruiting and onboarding costs. Add a $2,500 stay bonus at 12-month anniversary — costs $65K if everyone stays, saves $80K in turnover cost.
3. Clinical Scheduling Efficiency — $190K saved
The problem: Clinical payroll is $1.9M. Even 10% inefficiency from overtime, mismatched staffing-to-census, or manual scheduling errors = $190K wasted. With 36 clinical staff running 24/7 across 3 service lines with different acuity levels, this is a management complexity problem disguised as a payroll problem.

Step 1: Establish census-based staffing ratios by service line. Residential (3.5): 1 LPN per 8–10 patients. Detox (3.7): 1 LPN per 4–6 patients (higher acuity, medical monitoring). Halfway House (3.1): 1 Tech per 10–12 patients. Post these ratios and enforce them — on low-census days, staff go home early or use PTO.

Step 2: Publish 8-week rolling schedules. The #1 driver of call-outs is scheduling surprises. When staff know their schedule 6+ weeks ahead, personal conflicts get resolved proactively instead of becoming last-minute call-outs that trigger OT or agency.

Step 3: Cross-train Behavioral Health Techs to cover non-licensed functions currently being done by LPNs (vital signs documentation, patient transport, group facilitation support). This shifts some work off the $30.50/hr LPN rate to $14–17/hr Tech rate — same quality of care, lower blended labor cost.

OT math: If OT is currently 8% of clinical payroll ($152K/yr), cutting it to 3% saves $95K alone. The remaining $95K comes from census-matching staffing levels on a daily basis.
4. Food & Beverage (GPO) — $71K saved
The problem: $285,950/yr = ~$350/patient/month. Industry benchmark for residential SUD is $200–$280/patient/month. At 75 avg census, Stoa is running $12.50/patient/day vs. a well-run facility at $8–10/day. That gap is $75–110K/yr in excess food cost.

Step 1 (Week 1): Join a Group Purchasing Organization. Avendra (hotels/healthcare), Premier Health, and HealthTrust all serve behavioral health. Membership is free. Immediate savings: 10–20% on food, cleaning supplies, and disposables. Year 1 savings from GPO alone: $28K–$57K with zero operational change.

Step 2 (Month 1–2): Menu engineering. Build a 3-week rotating standardized menu with 2 approved substitutions per day. Benefits: enables bulk purchasing, reduces waste from over-ordering specialty items, simplifies kitchen labor. Target: kitchen staff should spend 80% of time cooking, 20% on prep/ordering — not the reverse.

Step 3 (Monthly): Track cost-per-patient-per-day as a KPI on the weekly ops dashboard. When kitchen staff know it's measured, it gets managed. Small changes (portion standardization, waste tracking) typically reduce food cost by additional 5–8% within 90 days.

Quick win: Call Sysco or US Foods today and ask for their healthcare GPO pricing deck. Takes 30 minutes. Most facilities save 8–12% on first order.
5. Legal & Professional — $50K saved
The problem: $150K/yr in legal and professional fees is high for a single-site operator. This likely includes outside counsel for employment matters, compliance consulting, and CPA/tax — all billed at hourly rates without a cap.

Action 1: Move to a flat-fee retainer with a healthcare-specialized firm. Jones Walker LLP and Baker Donelson both have Louisiana behavioral health practices. A $7,500/month all-in retainer covering employment, regulatory, and contract review = $90K/yr vs. likely $130K+ hourly. Savings: $40K.

Action 2: Bundle CPA and tax work. If OpCo and any related entities (property LLC, management company) are with separate firms, consolidating saves 15–25% on professional fees immediately.

Action 3: Implement an HR platform (Rippling, Gusto, or ADP Workforce Now) that automates employee handbook management, policy acknowledgments, and routine compliance tasks currently handled by outside HR consultants.
6. Travel — $45K saved
The problem: $90K in travel for a single facility in Baton Rouge is 1.2% of revenue — unusually high. This is almost certainly owner/executive travel without a written policy or approval process. Conference attendance, vendor site visits, and industry events can balloon without controls.

Action 1 (Day 1): Implement a written travel policy. All travel over $500 requires pre-approval. Economy class on flights under 4 hours. Hotel cap: $175/night (Baton Rouge market rate). Uber/Lyft for ground transport under 30 miles. No exceptions without CFO approval.

Action 2: Separate owner personal travel from business travel explicitly in the books. Any travel that benefits the owner's other businesses or personal interests should not run through Stoa's P&L — this is a PE audit red flag.

Action 3: Replace most conference attendance with virtual CEU platforms for clinical staff (NAADAC online, SAMHSA webinars are free). In-person conferences reserved for BD/relationship purposes only, and logged with expected referral pipeline impact.

▶ AI Implementation Playbook — How Each System Gets Deployed
🩹 Clinical Documentation AI — $120K/yr
Vendors: Nabla, DeepScribe, Nuance DAX · Integrates with EHR (Kipu, Netsmart, etc.)
The problem it solves: Stoa has 10 counselors and 26 LPNs writing progress notes, treatment plan updates, ASAM assessments, and discharge summaries. At an average of 2–3 hrs/day per clinician, documentation consumes 25–35% of their paid time. That's 6,500+ hours/year of clinical staff time spent typing instead of treating patients.

How AI documentation works: An AI ambient scribe (microphone in the session room) listens to clinician-patient interactions with consent, transcribes the conversation in real time, and auto-generates a structured clinical note mapped to your EHR's fields — SOAP format, treatment goals, DSM-5 diagnosis codes, progress against goals. The clinician reviews the draft (takes 3–5 minutes instead of 30) and clicks approve.

Deployment path:
  • Month 1: Pilot with 3 volunteer counselors. Measure time-per-note before (baseline) vs. after. Target: 75% time reduction.
  • Month 2: Expand to all 10 counselors + nursing daily notes. Measure adoption rate and note quality (compliance coordinator reviews sample notes weekly).
  • Month 3+: Use AI to flag incomplete documentation before claim submission. Every missing note = a denied claim. This directly ties documentation AI to billing revenue.
The real value: Clinicians reclaim 1.5–2 hrs/day. They don't leave early — they carry more patients. At $65K avg counselor salary, 1.5 hrs/day redirected to patient care = capacity to serve 15–20% more patients without hiring. That's the revenue multiplier that makes this an 8–10x ROI investment, not a 1.7x.
💵 AI-Powered RCM & Billing — $145K/yr
Vendors: Waystar, Availity, Netsmart RCM · Clean claim rate target: 95%+
The problem it solves: The current outsourced billing vendor charges 5% of collections. But the hidden cost is worse: behavioral health billing nationally averages a 10–15% initial denial rate. Each denied claim costs $25–$150 in rework time, and 15–20% of denied claims are never reworked and never paid. On $7.26M revenue, that's potentially $90K–$180K in uncollected revenue annually — on top of the $363K fee.

How AI RCM works: Before a claim is submitted, AI scrubs it against payer-specific rules (every insurance company has different coding requirements, pre-auth rules, and timely filing limits). Claims with a high predicted denial probability are flagged for human review before submission. AI also manages the prior authorization queue — sending requests, following up, and escalating overdue auths that are holding up admissions.

Deployment path:
  • Week 1–4: Data audit. Pull 90-day A/R aging, denial reason codes (top 5 denial codes = where to start), and average days-to-collect by payer. You cannot improve what you don't measure.
  • Month 2: Layer AI claim scrubbing onto existing billing workflow — no vendor change yet. This immediately lifts clean claim rate from ~78% to 92%+ and reduces denial rework cost.
  • Month 4–6: Evaluate bringing billing in-house. One certified behavioral health biller (CBHC credential) at $55K salary + $60K AI platform = $115K fully-loaded vs. $363K outsourced. Saves $248K/yr. Transition risk: budget 6 months for full handoff.
Payer mix matters: Commercial insurance pays 2–4x Medicaid rates for the same service. If even 10% of Stoa's census shifts from Medicaid to commercial, that's $300–500K in incremental revenue with the same cost structure. AI admission triage tools can pre-screen insurance coverage and prioritize commercial admits for available beds.
📅 AI Staff Scheduling — $95K/yr
Vendors: Symplr (API Healthcare), Shiftboard, When I Work AI · Integrates with payroll
The problem it solves: 41 staff across 24/7 operations with different certification requirements by shift and service line. Manual scheduling by a supervisor takes 3–5 hrs/week. Last-minute call-outs trigger agency fills at $50–70/hr vs. $30.50/hr for employees — a 65–130% premium. One agency shift per day = $70K/yr in excess labor cost.

How AI scheduling works: The platform ingests staff availability, certifications (each LPN's license expiry, CPR status, ASAM training level), shift preferences, labor law constraints (break requirements, max consecutive hours), and historical call-out patterns. It generates optimal schedules 2–3 weeks in advance and pushes them to staff via mobile app. Staff swap requests go through the app and auto-check qualification requirements before approving.

Deployment path:
  • Month 1: Data entry phase. Input all 41 staff profiles: certifications, available hours, preferred shifts, payroll classification. Connect to payroll system (ADP/Paychex) via API for automatic hours sync. This is a one-time 8–10 hour setup task.
  • Month 2: Run AI-suggested schedules in parallel with current manual process. Supervisor reviews and approves. Track OT hours as primary KPI — expect 20–30% OT reduction in first 60 days just from better advance planning.
  • Month 3+: Enable predictive mode. AI flags coverage gaps appearing on the horizon (e.g., 3 LPNs requesting same weekend off, or historically high call-out period coming). Proactive float pool activation costs 40% less than reactive agency calls.
Detox-specific benefit: 3.7 Detox requires 1 RN per 4–6 patients for medical monitoring. As detox census fluctuates (0→10 beds in the model), manual scheduling can't respond fast enough. AI scheduling dynamically adjusts nursing ratios daily based on actual admissions and discharges — preventing both unsafe under-staffing and expensive over-staffing.
📊 Predictive Census Management — $80K cost savings + $1.5M revenue upside
Tools: Kipu Health Analytics, custom Power BI/Tableau dashboard, or Salesforce Health Cloud · Free to build initially
The problem it solves: Stoa is at 62% occupancy. That means 38 beds are empty every night, each generating $0. At $335/day avg rate, one empty bed = $122K/yr in unrealized revenue. The difference between 62% and 80% occupancy is $1.5M in additional annual revenue — with near-zero incremental cost since fixed costs are already covered.

How predictive census management works: The system tracks real-time bed availability by service line, forecasts discharges 7–14 days ahead (based on typical length-of-stay by payer type), alerts admissions when beds are opening, and feeds that information to the marketing/BDR team so they can activate referral sources proactively. It also flags patients at high risk for AMA (against medical advice) discharge based on behavioral indicators — early intervention can improve retention rates 15–25%.

Deployment path:
  • Month 1 (Free phase): Build a simple daily census Google Sheet with auto-calculated occupancy rate by service line. This alone, reviewed in daily standup, creates accountability and visibility that typically improves occupancy 3–5% within 30 days.
  • Month 2–3: Add referral source tracking. Log every inquiry call, admission, and referral source. Within 60 days you'll know which 5–7 referral relationships drive 70% of admissions. Those become the BDR team's priority targets.
  • Month 4+: Layer predictive analytics — 12–18 months of historical admission/discharge data is enough to build a reliable 14-day forecast model. When projected census drops below 75% for the next 10 days, auto-trigger outreach to top referral sources via CRM.
Highest-leverage initiative in this entire analysis. Every 1% occupancy gain at current rates = $109K/yr revenue. The cost of a basic census dashboard: $0. The cost of a proper predictive system: $25–30K/yr. Payback on the first additional admission: 3 days.
📢 AI Marketing & Referral Engine — $75K attributed + $500K–$1.5M revenue
Tools: HubSpot Healthcare (free tier to start), Salesforce Health Cloud, Google Ads, Meta Ads · Current marketing budget: $12K/yr
The problem it solves: Stoa spends $12,000/year on marketing for a $7.26M business — that's 0.17% of revenue. Healthcare industry benchmark is 2–5% of revenue ($145K–$363K for Stoa). The entire patient pipeline runs on personal relationships, with zero system, zero tracking, and zero ability to predict or influence census. This is the most concentrated risk in the business.

How AI-driven referral management works: A CRM (HubSpot Healthcare or Salesforce Health Cloud) tracks every referral source: which hospital discharge planners, drug courts, probation officers, EAPs, primary care physicians, and former patients send referrals. AI scores each source by volume, quality, and recency. The system auto-routes follow-up tasks to the BDR, sends check-in emails to warm referral sources, and alerts when a high-value referrer goes quiet for 30+ days.

Deployment path:
  • Month 1 — The $70K hire: Director of Business Development. This is the single highest-ROI hire available to Stoa. Their job: visit hospital discharge planners at Our Lady of the Lake, Baton Rouge General, and Lane Regional every week. Build relationships with drug court coordinators in EBR and surrounding parishes. Attend community recovery meetings as an industry representative. A good BDR generates 15–20 additional admissions in Year 1 = $500K–$700K revenue on a $70K + benefits investment.
  • Month 2 — CRM setup: Load all existing contacts. Log every referral received in the past 12 months from memory. Start tracking inquiry-to-admission conversion rate (industry benchmark: 30–40%). If Stoa is below that, there's a conversion process problem, not just a volume problem.
  • Month 3 — Digital: Google Ads targeting "drug rehab Baton Rouge," "alcohol treatment Louisiana," "detox near me." Budget: $4–6K/month. Behavioral health Google Ads convert at 3–8% of clicks. At $12/click and 3% conversion, each admission costs ~$400 in ad spend vs. $335 avg daily rate = paid back on Day 2 of each admission.
Critical risk note: If 2–3 of Stoa's current referral relationships end (discharge planner changes hospitals, court coordinator moves on), census could drop 20–30% overnight. Building a diversified, tracked referral network is the most important risk mitigation action in this entire memo — more important than any cost cut.
📋 Compliance Automation — $60K/yr (risk value: $500K–$2M)
Tools: Qualifacts, Accreditation Guru, SimplePractice Compliance, or MedBridge · Behavioral health specific
The problem it solves: Stoa operates under LADPH licensing, likely CARF accreditation, and must maintain compliance with 26 LPN license renewals, DEA schedule II-V registrations for detox, CPR/First Aid certifications, HIPAA policies, and payer credentialing for each insurance contract. Manual tracking of this is a full-time job. One missed LPN license renewal = up to $10K fine in Louisiana and potential immediate citation. One failed CARF survey = provisional status, which triggers payer audits that can halt payments for 30–90 days — a $600K–$1.8M cash flow event.

How compliance automation works: The platform maintains a master compliance calendar for every staff member (license expiry, training due dates, policy acknowledgments) and every regulatory requirement (monthly medication counts, quarterly fire drills, annual CARF mock surveys). Automated alerts fire 90/60/30/7 days before any expiry. Policy updates are distributed digitally with timestamped acknowledgment — eliminating "we didn't know" citations.

Deployment path:
  • Week 1 (free): Build a Google Sheet with every staff member's license number, expiry date, and renewal cost. Sort by expiry date. Anything expiring in the next 90 days is a fire drill. This alone prevents the most common and expensive compliance failures.
  • Month 2: Implement a digital policy management system. Every time a policy is updated (HIPAA, medication management, patient rights), it routes to all relevant staff for e-sign acknowledgment. Creates a defensible paper trail for any audit.
  • Month 3–6: Run a mock CARF audit using their published standards checklist. Identify gaps. Fix them before the real survey. Facilities that do annual mock audits score 40% better on actual surveys and spend 70% less on compliance consulting.
PE diligence note: Any behavioral health acquisition requires a compliance audit in the first 30 days. Known compliance gaps are purchase price adjustments. Unknown compliance gaps discovered post-close are lawsuits. A clean compliance program adds $1–2M to the acquisition valuation conversation.
