2. Contract Labor — $126K saved
The problem: $246K/yr in agency/contract labor = ~12–13% of clinical payroll. Agency rates for LPNs in Louisiana run $50–70/hr vs. $30.50/hr for direct employees — a 65–130% premium. This is the "fire department" problem: you're paying emergency prices for routine coverage.

Step 1 (Month 1): Audit which shifts are consistently filled by agency. Is it always weekend nights? Specific positions? If it's predictable, it's solvable with better planning — not emergency agency calls.

Step 2 (Month 1–3): Build an internal per diem float pool. Hire 2–3 per diem LPNs at $35/hr (no benefits required for under 30hrs/week) who commit to 2 weekend shifts/month. Upfront cost: ~$5K in sign-on bonuses. Annual savings vs. agency: $80K+.

Step 3 (Month 2–4): Partner with LSU School of Nursing and Southern University at Baton Rouge for clinical placements. Nursing students cover non-licensed tasks under supervision — free labor for patient transport, vitals, documentation support during peak hours.

Step 4 (Ongoing): Retention program. LPN turnover nationally runs 30–40%/yr. At Stoa's LPN count, that's 8–10 replacements/year at $5–8K each in recruiting and onboarding costs. Add a $2,500 stay bonus at 12-month anniversary — costs $65K if everyone stays, saves $80K in turnover cost.
3. Clinical Scheduling Efficiency — $190K saved
The problem: Clinical payroll is $1.9M. Even 10% inefficiency from overtime, mismatched staffing-to-census, or manual scheduling errors = $190K wasted. With 36 clinical staff running 24/7 across 3 service lines with different acuity levels, this is a management complexity problem disguised as a payroll problem.

Step 1: Establish census-based staffing ratios by service line. Residential (3.5): 1 LPN per 8–10 patients. Detox (3.7): 1 LPN per 4–6 patients (higher acuity, medical monitoring). Halfway House (3.1): 1 Tech per 10–12 patients. Post these ratios and enforce them — on low-census days, staff go home early or use PTO.

Step 2: Publish 8-week rolling schedules. The #1 driver of call-outs is scheduling surprises. When staff know their schedule 6+ weeks ahead, personal conflicts get resolved proactively instead of becoming last-minute call-outs that trigger OT or agency.

Step 3: Cross-train Behavioral Health Techs to cover non-licensed functions currently being done by LPNs (vital signs documentation, patient transport, group facilitation support). This shifts some work off the $30.50/hr LPN rate to $14–17/hr Tech rate — same quality of care, lower blended labor cost.

OT math: If OT is currently 8% of clinical payroll ($152K/yr), cutting it to 3% saves $95K alone. The remaining $95K comes from census-matching staffing levels on a daily basis.

Immediate savings: 10–20% on food, cleaning supplies, and disposables. Year 1 savings from GPO alone: $28K–$57K with zero operational change.

Step 2 (Month 1–2): Menu engineering. Build a 3-week rotating standardized menu with 2 approved substitutions per day. Benefits: enables bulk purchasing, reduces waste from over-ordering specialty items, simplifies kitchen labor. Target: kitchen staff should spend 80% of time cooking, 20% on prep/ordering — not the reverse.

Step 3 (Monthly): Track cost-per-patient-per-day as a KPI on the weekly ops dashboard. When kitchen staff know it's measured, it gets managed. Small changes (portion standardization, waste tracking) typically reduce food cost by additional 5–8% within 90 days.

Quick win: Call Sysco or US Foods today and ask for their healthcare GPO pricing deck. Takes 30 minutes. Most facilities save 8–12% on first order.

The problem: $90K in travel for a single facility in Baton Rouge is 1.2% of revenue — unusually high. This is almost certainly owner/executive travel without a written policy or approval process. Conference attendance, vendor site visits, and industry events can balloon without controls.

Action 1 (Day 1): Implement a written travel policy. All travel over $500 requires pre-approval. Economy class on flights under 4 hours. Hotel cap: $175/night (Baton Rouge market rate). Uber/Lyft for ground transport under 30 miles. No exceptions without CFO approval.

Action 2: Separate owner personal travel from business travel explicitly in the books. Any travel that benefits the owner's other businesses or personal interests should not run through Stoa's P&L — this is a PE audit red flag.

Action 3: Replace most conference attendance with virtual CEU platforms for clinical staff (NAADAC online, SAMHSA webinars are free). In-person conferences reserved for BD/relationship purposes only, and logged with expected referral pipeline impact.

AI Implementation Playbook — How Each System Gets Deployed
🩹 Clinical Documentation AI — $120K/yr
Vendors: Nabla, DeepScribe, Nuance DAX · Integrates with EHR (Kipu, Netsmart, etc.)
The problem it solves: Stoa has 10 counselors and 26 LPNs writing progress notes, treatment plan updates, ASAM assessments, and discharge summaries. At an average of 2–3 hrs/day per clinician, documentation consumes 25–35% of their paid time. That's 6,500+ hours/year of clinical staff time spent typing instead of treating patients.

How AI documentation works: An AI ambient scribe (microphone in the session room) listens to clinician-patient interactions with consent, transcribes the conversation in real time, and auto-generates a structured clinical note mapped to your EHR's fields — SOAP format, treatment goals, DSM-5 diagnosis codes, progress against goals. The clinician reviews the draft (takes 3–5 minutes instead of 30) and clicks approve.

Deployment path:
  • Month 1: Pilot with 3 volunteer counselors. Measure time-per-note before (baseline) vs. after. Target: 75% time reduction.
  • Month 2: Expand to all 10 counselors + nursing daily notes. Measure adoption rate and note quality (compliance coordinator reviews sample notes weekly).
  • Month 3+: Use AI to flag incomplete documentation before claim submission. Every missing note = a denied claim. This directly ties documentation AI to billing revenue.
The real value: Clinicians reclaim 1.5–2 hrs/day. They don't leave early — they carry more patients. At $65K avg counselor salary, 1.5 hrs/day redirected to patient care = capacity to serve 15–20% more patients without hiring. That's the revenue multiplier that makes this an 8–10x ROI investment, not a 1.7x.
💵 AI-Powered RCM & Billing — $145K/yr
Vendors: Waystar, Availity, Netsmart RCM · Clean claim rate target: 95%+
Actual A/R data (April 2026): Collection rate 94.9% YTD · Total A/R outstanding: $563K · At-risk 90+ days: $264K (46.9% of A/R) · High-risk 120+ days: $215K

The two problems AI RCM solves:
(1) Cost structure: Paying 5% on $7.26M = $363K/yr for 94.9% performance. That rate justifies 3–3.5% at most. AI-assisted claim scrubbing + renegotiation = $109K savings immediately.
(2) The $215K 120+ day problem: This bucket is not a billing quality issue — it's a follow-up and appeals management issue. AI RCM automates denial tracking, appeal letter generation, and timely filing deadline monitoring. These are exactly the claims that fall through the cracks with manual billing. Estimated recovery from active AI-driven rework: $85K–$130K on the existing 120+ bucket alone — ROI on the $60K platform cost in the first month.

How AI RCM works: Before a claim is submitted, AI scrubs it against payer-specific rules (every insurance company has different coding requirements, pre-auth rules, and timely filing limits). Claims with a high predicted denial probability are flagged for human review before submission. AI also manages the prior authorization queue — sending requests, following up, and escalating overdue auths that are holding up admissions.

Deployment path:
  • Week 1–4: Data audit. Pull 90-day A/R aging, denial reason codes (top 5 denial codes = where to start), and average days-to-collect by payer. You cannot improve what you don't measure.
  • Month 2: Layer AI claim scrubbing onto existing billing workflow — no vendor change yet. This immediately lifts clean claim rate from ~78% to 92%+ and reduces denial rework cost.
  • Month 4–6: Evaluate bringing billing in-house. One certified behavioral health biller (CBHC credential) at $55K salary + $60K AI platform = $115K fully-loaded vs. $363K outsourced. Saves $248K/yr. Transition risk: budget 6 months for full handoff.
Payer mix matters: Commercial insurance pays 2–4x Medicaid rates for the same service. If even 10% of Stoa's census shifts from Medicaid to commercial, that's $300–500K in incremental revenue with the same cost structure. AI admission triage tools can pre-screen insurance coverage and prioritize commercial admits for available beds.
📅 AI Staff Scheduling — $95K/yr

How AI scheduling works: The platform ingests staff availability, certifications (each LPN's license expiry, CPR status, ASAM training level), shift preferences, labor law constraints (break requirements, max consecutive hours), and historical call-out patterns. AI scheduling dynamically adjusts nursing ratios daily based on actual admissions and discharges — preventing both unsafe under-staffing and expensive over-staffing.
📊 Predictive Census Management — $80K cost savings + $1.5M revenue upside

The problem it solves: Stoa is at 84% occupancy (94 of 104 beds). That means ~10 beds are still empty every night, each generating $0. At $335/day avg rate, one empty bed = $122K/yr in unrealized revenue. The difference between 90% and 97% occupancy is ~$680K in additional annual revenue (each bed = $122K/yr at $335/day) — with near-zero incremental cost since fixed costs are already covered.

How predictive census management works: The system tracks real-time bed availability by service line, forecasts discharges 7–14 days ahead (based on typical length-of-stay by payer type), alerts admissions when beds are opening, and feeds that information to the marketing/BDR team so they can activate referral sources proactively. Deployment path:

  • Month 4+: Layer predictive analytics — 12–18 months of historical admission/discharge data is enough to build a reliable 14-day forecast model. When projected census drops below 75% for the next 10 days, auto-trigger outreach to top referral sources via CRM.
Highest-leverage initiative in this entire analysis. Every 1% occupancy gain at current rates = $109K/yr revenue. The cost of a basic census dashboard: $0. The cost of a proper predictive system: $25–30K/yr. Payback on the first additional admission: 3 days.

What the Waiver Does
Federal Medicaid law contains the IMD Exclusion — a rule that prohibits Medicaid from paying for services provided to patients aged 21–64 in any facility with more than 16 beds primarily engaged in SUD or mental health treatment.

Stoa has 104 licensed beds. Without the waiver, Medicaid pays $0 for any residential or detox patient stay. The 91% Medicaid payer mix would collapse to near zero billable revenue on the residential census.

Louisiana's Healthy Louisiana OUD/SUD 1115 Demonstration Waiver waives the IMD exclusion, allowing CMS to reimburse Louisiana Medicaid for SUD treatment services — including residential (3.5) and detox (3.7) — in facilities like Stoa. This waiver is the legal and financial permission slip for the entire business model.

Renewal Risk Assessment
Why renewal is likely (HIGH probability):
  • SUD/IMD waivers were a priority of the first Trump administration — he expanded them. Current Trump CMS has been rolling back other waiver types (continuous enrollment, HRSN) but has NOT targeted SUD waivers.
  • The opioid crisis is bipartisan. Louisiana has deep Republican support for treatment-based approaches (vs. purely criminal justice).
  • Louisiana already ran this waiver for 9+ years with clean compliance reports. CMS renewal applications for functioning waivers are rarely denied.
  • LDH active engagement: October 2025 forum confirms ongoing reporting and milestone compliance — no findings that would threaten renewal.
Residual risks:
  • Federal Medicaid block grant legislation (proposed in 2025 reconciliation bills) could change the entire funding structure — not waiver-specific, but would affect all Medicaid-dependent providers.
  • If LDH submits renewal application late or with compliance deficiencies, there could be a gap in waiver coverage. Stoa should confirm LDH's renewal timeline directly.
STC Compliance Requirements (Stoa's Obligations)
To maintain waiver eligibility, Stoa must remain in good standing with Louisiana's SUD waiver Special Terms and Conditions (STCs):
  • Medicaid Managed Care contracts: Must be credentialed with all 5 Healthy Louisiana MCOs (Aetna Better Health, AmeriHealth Caritas, Humana, UnitedHealthcare Community Plan, BCBS LA). Any lapse = denied claims.
  • ASAM level of care criteria: Must use ASAM criteria to justify each admission level. Detox (3.7) requires documented medical withdrawal necessity. Residential (3.5) requires LOCUS/ASAM criteria. Missing documentation = automatic denial.
  • Prior authorization: Each admission requires prior auth from the patient's MCO before day 1. Failure to obtain auth = uncovered stay. This is likely the primary cause of the $215K 120+ day A/R.
  • Discharge planning: Waiver requires documented step-down planning. Connects directly to the alumni/IOP revenue opportunity.
  • Quality metrics: LDH requires quarterly reporting on readmission rates, LOI, completion rates. These must stay within approved ranges.
PE diligence item: Request Stoa's MCO credentialing status for all 5 Healthy Louisiana plans + last 3 prior auth denial rates by service line. This tells you the billing engine's true health.

1. Commercial Mix Diversification
HIGHEST IMPACT
The permanent fix. If commercial/private-pay mix reaches 25–30%, waiver expiry becomes a survivable event — not an existential one. At 30% commercial, Medicaid-sourced revenue drops to 70% of the total, and the business can operate at meaningful profitability even in a worst-case waiver gap.

How:
  • Hire a commercial-focused BDR specifically targeting: Employee Assistance Programs (EAPs), self-insured employers in Baton Rouge MSA (ExxonMobil, Turner Industries, Cajun Industries), private health insurance brokers, and executive/professional referral networks. Commercial patients don't come from hospital discharge planners — they come from EAPs and employer channels.
  • Detox (3.7) is your commercial wedge. Medical withdrawal management attracts commercially insured patients (higher-income, working adults seeking confidential treatment). At $482/day commercial rate vs. Medicaid's much lower rate, 5–8 commercial detox beds generates disproportionate revenue impact.
  • Private-pay concierge track: Reserve 5–10 beds as a premium private-pay program ($600–$800/day all-in). Amenities: private room, family therapy, aftercare coordination. Margin: 80%+. No insurance bureaucracy. Target: professionals, executives, legal referrals.
2. Proactive Waiver Renewal Engagement
START NOW
Don't wait for 2027. Louisiana's renewal application cycle typically begins 12–18 months before expiry (mid-2026). The application must demonstrate: measurable outcomes (readmission rates, completion rates, LOI), access improvements, and cost-effectiveness vs. alternatives.

Actions:
  • Engage LDH's Office of Behavioral Health (OBH) directly within 90 days of close. Introduce ownership, confirm credentialing status, and understand the renewal timeline and any emerging compliance concerns.
  • Build a data story now. Track and document: patient outcomes (30/60/90-day sobriety rates), reduction in ER visits post-discharge, completion rates vs. AMA. This is exactly what CMS evaluates in renewal applications. Starting data collection now means 12–18 months of outcome data by renewal time.
  • Engage a Medicaid policy consultant (Manatt, Phelps & Phillips or Sellers Dorsey) who specializes in Louisiana 1115 renewals. Cost: $25–50K. Insurance against a $6.6M/yr revenue risk.
3. Contractual Deal Protections
IN THE LOI
Price the risk into the acquisition structure. No PE firm should close a 91% Medicaid business 20 months from a waiver cliff without contractual protection.

4. Service Line Restructuring for Waiver Independence
YEAR 1–2
Build revenue lines that don't need the IMD waiver at all. The waiver only covers residential IMD stays. Outpatient services (IOP, PHP) are covered under standard Medicaid — no waiver required.

How to reduce IMD dependency:
  • Expand IOP/PHP aggressively. Outpatient services = Medicaid standard plan benefit (no IMD waiver needed). Building IOP from $600K → $1.5M/yr reduces waiver-dependent revenue concentration from 91% to ~80%. Each IOP slot also generates alumni referrals back to residential.
  • Telehealth IOP: Telehealth behavioral health is a Medicaid-covered service in Louisiana with no IMD restriction. Launch a virtual IOP program serving statewide population — not just Baton Rouge. $180/session × 15 sessions/week × 30 patients = $1.2M/yr with near-zero marginal cost after setup.
  • MAT clinic (outpatient): Suboxone/Vivitrol prescribing is outpatient — standard Medicaid benefit, no waiver. Adds $500K–$800K/yr and is completely waiver-independent revenue.
5. Federal Legislative Insurance — Advocate for Permanent IMD Fix
LOW COST / LONG-TERM
The ultimate hedge: eliminate the IMD exclusion entirely. Multiple bills have been introduced in Congress to permanently repeal or substantially modify the IMD exclusion for SUD/behavioral health facilities. The TREAT Act (bipartisan) has been reintroduced repeatedly and has significant support. If passed, the 1115 waiver becomes irrelevant — Medicaid simply covers IMD stays permanently.

Actions:
  • Join NAADAC, NAATP, and the Louisiana Association of Substance Abuse Counselors (LASACT) — these are the lobbying organizations that advocate for IMD exclusion repeal. Cost: $2–5K/yr dues.
  • Participate in CMS and LDH comment periods on waiver renewals and ASAM fee schedule updates. Documented provider support strengthens the state's renewal application and positions Stoa as a model provider — not just a beneficiary.
Bottom line: Strategies 1–4 are the operational hedge. Strategy 5 is the nuclear option — if the IMD exclusion is permanently repealed, Stoa's 91% Medicaid mix becomes a competitive advantage (established payer relationships, proven outcomes, first-mover scale) rather than a risk factor. The valuation re-rates to 10–12× overnight.
